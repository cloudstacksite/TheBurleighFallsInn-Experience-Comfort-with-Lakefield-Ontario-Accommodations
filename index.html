The Burleigh Falls Inn: A Historic Retreat

Location and Scenic Beauty

Just a short drive from Lakefield, The Burleigh Falls Inn is perched on an 11-acre island where the waters of Lovesick and Stoney Lakes converge. This idyllic location is a dream for nature lovers, offering panoramic views of the water, lush greenery, and an atmosphere of serene relaxation. Guests are treated to stunning sunrises over Stoney Lake and breathtaking sunsets over Lovesick Lake, making every moment unforgettable.

Accommodations and Amenities

The Burleigh Falls Inn is more than just a place to stay; it’s an experience. With its rich history and modern comforts, the Inn offers a variety of accommodations tailored to different preferences:

  • Rooms and Suites: Each room combines rustic charm with contemporary amenities. From cozy single rooms to expansive suites, there’s something for every traveller.
  • Dining at the Falls: Guests can savour a culinary journey with dishes inspired by local flavours. Breakfast, lunch, and dinner are served with a side of stunning lake views.
  • Event Hosting: The Inn’s versatile event spaces make it popular for weddings, corporate gatherings, and family reunions.
  • Live Entertainment: Weekly events such as live music and comedy nights ensure never dull moments.
  • Outdoor Activities: The Inn’s proximity to natural attractions makes it perfect for fishing, boating, hiking, and bird-watching.

Packages and Special Offers

The Burleigh Falls Inn frequently offers special packages to enhance your stay. From romantic escapes with champagne and private dinners to adventure-filled weekends featuring guided tours and activities, there’s something to suit every type of traveller.

Things to Do Around Lakefield Ontario Accommodations

Lakefield isn’t just a place to stay—it’s a destination packed with activities and attractions. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or someone who loves to shop, Lakefield has something for you.

Explore the Trent-Severn Waterway

The historic Trent-Severn Waterway is a must-visit for boating enthusiasts and history buffs. This iconic canal system connects Lakefield to other parts of the Kawarthas, offering scenic boat rides and a glimpse into Canada’s engineering heritage.

Whether you’re kayaking, canoeing, or cruising, the waterway provides endless opportunities for exploration. Be sure to pack a camera to capture the stunning landscapes along the way.

Discover Local Artisans

Lakefield is home to a vibrant arts community. Visitors can explore galleries and studios showcasing handmade crafts, paintings, and sculptures inspired by the Kawarthas’ natural beauty.

Don’t miss the chance to pick up a one-of-a-kind souvenir to remember your trip. Many local artisans also host workshops, giving you the chance to create your own masterpiece.

Outdoor Recreation

For outdoor enthusiasts, Lakefield is a paradise.

Some popular activities include:

  • Hiking Trails: Explore trails that wind through forests, wetlands, and along lakeshores. The Kawartha Trans Canada Trail is a local favorite.
  • Fishing Spots: The region’s lakes and rivers are teeming with fish, making it a hotspot for anglers. Be sure to check local guidelines and permits.
  • Cycling Routes: Enjoy scenic bike rides through the countryside with routes suitable for both casual cyclists and experienced riders.
  • Winter Activities: In the colder months, visitors can enjoy snowshoeing, cross-country skiing, and ice fishing.

Seasonal Festivals

Lakefield hosts a variety of festivals throughout the year, celebrating everything from local food and music to arts and crafts. These events offer a fantastic way to immerse yourself in the community and experience the region’s culture.

Popular festivals include the annual “Lakefield Jazz, Art & Craft Festival” and winter holiday markets.

Planning Your Stay in Lakefield

When planning your visit, consider these tips to make the most of your trip and enjoy the best of Lakefield Ontario accommodations:
